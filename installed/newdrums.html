Adding new drum or loop sounds

You can add your own drum or loop sounds to the program, or sounds that you've downloaded from the Web.

First ensure that your sounds are 16-bit uncompressed WAV files. They can be either mono or stereo, and at any sampling frequency (we recommend 44 kHz - if you use another frequency it is converted to 44 kHz internally). You can use any sound editing program to convert their format if necessary.

Then drag the WAV files into the appropriate folder. leafDrums has two folders, one for drums and one for loops. You can open a window on the folders by selecting "drum" or "loop" on the leafDrums display, and then clicking the folder icon just to the right.

Once you've dragged the WAV file into the right folder, it should appear on the list of available sounds. You can now select it to use in a drum pattern.
