Tweaking drum or loop sounds

Drum or loop sounds can both be customised slightly to suit the rhythm you are creating.

Drum sounds

When you select a drum sound, three scrollbars below the list of drum sounds become active.

These allow you to alter the length of the sound - in other words, you can cut off the end - and to control how quickly the sound comes in at its beginning and goes out at its end. By default, the sound begins and ends playing completely abruptly, going from nothing to full volume instantly and vice versa.

Adjusting these sliders allows you to alter the attack and decay of some sounds (how well it works depends on the samples involved, but it is especially useful with "open hi-hat" cymbal sounds).

Loops

With loops there is only one control - a slider for the number of beats. You can make the loop any length between one time-unit and 16 time units (i.e. up to 4 beats or 1 bar). The loop will be automatically stretched to fill that many beats, whatever the tempo. (This results in changes in pitch.)
